DNA mismatch base pair

A mismatched base pair occurs when a sequence is paired with a sequence that is partially complementary for binding to desired chains, but not unintended chains. When chains are joined into two strands, there are many combinations that complement each of the chains; accordingly, they are linked, and the combination is intentionally reduced to prevent combining. For example, strands 1 and 2, which are unwanted bonds, replacement with base sequences that are partially noncomplementary results in a decrease in complementary portions, thereby reducing binding.
