DNA mismatch base pair

A mismatched base pair occurs when a sequence is paired with a sequence that is partially complementary for binding to desired chains, but not unintended chains. When chains are joined into two strands, there are many combinations that complement each of the chains; accordingly, they are linked and the combination is intentionally reduced to prevent combining. Design of DNA origami

This structure is a combination of a DNA Walker and DNA origami digitized in plate form. The Walker moves in one direction so that the connected plate-like DNA origami moves in a sliding manner. Furthermore, since the amount of movement of the structure depends on the amount of movement of the Walker, it can be adjusted. A design drawn using CADNano [1] demonstrates this point. Figure 1 shows a structure (roof) in which a walker is connected to a plate-like DNA origin. Figure 2 shows a structure (referred to as a floor) in which a single-stranded DNA serving as a foot of the Walker connected to a plate-like Origami. Figure 3 is a moving image simulated by Cando [2] of these design drawings. By combining these concepts, we believe that it is possible to realize a structure of DNA that can control the amount of movement by an input.
