  1. DNA mismatch base pair

    ミスマッチ塩基対とは望む鎖同士は結合しつつも、意図しない鎖同士が結合をしないように部分的に相補でない配列に置き換えることを言う。 鎖が結合して２本鎖になる時、それぞれの鎖で相補となる組み合わせが多いので結合するのだから、その組み合わせを意図的に減らして結合しなくするという仕組みである。 例えば、表に示すような仕様を満たす塩基配列を考えるとする。 〇と書いてある組み合わせは結合することを表し、×と書いてある組み合わせは結合しないことを表している。 望まない結合であるS1とS4において、一部相補でない塩基配列に置き換えることで相補の部分を減少させ結合しないようにする。

    A mismatched base pair means replacing a desired sequence with a sequence which is not partially complementary so as to bind desired chains but not unintended chains. When chains are joined into two strands, since there are many combinations that complement each of the chains, they are linked, so that the combination is intentionally reduced so as not to combine them. For example, consider a base sequence that satisfies the specifications shown in the table. A combination written ◯ indicates that the combination is to be combined, and a combination written as X indicates that the combination is not combined. In S1 and S4 which are unwanted bonds, by replacing them with base sequences which are not partially complementary, complementary portions are decreased so as not to bind.

  2. Design of DNA origami

    This structure is a combination of DNA Walker and DNA origami digitized in plate form. Walker moves in one direction so that the connected plate-like DNA origami is moved in a sliding manner. Furthermore, since the amount of movement of the structure depends on the amount of movement of Walker, it can be adjusted. A design drawing by caDNAno[2] is shown below. Fig. 1 is a design drawing of a structure (roof) in which a walker is connected to a plate-like DNA origin. Fig. 2 is a design drawing of a structure (referred to as a floor) in which a single-stranded DNA serving as a foot of Walker is connected to a plate-like Origami. Fig. 3 is a moving image simulated by CanDo[3] of these design drawings. By combining these, we believe that it is possible to realize a structure of DNA that can control the amount of movement by input.
