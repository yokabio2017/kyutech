The Transporter

Abstract

We propose a method for the development of DNA nanostructures that autonomously move in a single direction. A structure called a DNAWalker was used in which two DNA strands were partially bound and a single-stranded region was present at both ends of the double-stranded portion. The Walker runs on double-stranded DNA (Ground) in which single-stranded DNA (Spot) of the same base sequence with a hairpin structure is continuously arranged. The Walker uses its single-strand region as a toehold, causes a strand displacement reaction at a particular Spot, and moves to the next Spot. Then, the strand displacement reaction between the Walker and Spot takes place again and the Walker spontaneously travels on the Ground. Since the progress of the Walker depends on the initial concentration of fuel DNA, progress can be controlled by adjusting the initial concentration. We added mismatched base pairs to the nucleotide sequence of the DNAWalker to avoid an unintended reaction pathway. In addition, we changed the structure of the Walker to connect to the DNA origami to make it available as a force to move the DNA origami structure. Project Idea

We created a system that functions as a power source for moving DNA structures. We considered a system with a separate structure to be moved and power source, rather than one in which the DNA structure to be moved and its power source are united. In this framework, even if the DNA structure was changed according to the specifications of the application, movement was possible as long as a connection with the power source was made. Therefore, the aim of this project was to design a system that functions as a power source. The proposed power source must satisfy the following three specifications. First, this system must be able to connect to the target DNA structure. Second, since DNA molecules are in Brownian motion in solution, they move in all directions; therefore, this system should move in a single direction. Third, this system must be able to adjust its progress. We built a system that satisfies these three specifications with reference to the principle of operation of a structure called a DNAWalker [2].
