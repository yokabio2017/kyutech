ABSTRACT

We propose a method for the development of DNA nanostructures that autonomously move in a single direction. A structure called a DNAWalker was used in which two DNA strands were partially bound and a single-stranded region was present at both ends of the double-stranded portion. The Walker runs on double-stranded DNA (Ground) in which single-stranded DNA (Spot) of the same base sequence with a hairpin structure is continuously arranged. The Walker uses its single-strand region as a toehold, causes a strand displacement reaction at a particular Spot, and moves to the next Spot. Then, the strand displacement reaction between the Walker and Spot takes place again and the Walker spontaneously travels on the Ground. Since the progress of the Walker depends on the initial concentration of fuel DNA, progress can be controlled by adjusting the initial concentration. We added mismatched base pairs to the nucleotide sequence of the DNAWalker to avoid an unintended reaction pathway. In addition, we changed the structure of the Walker to connect to the DNA origami to make it available as a force to move the DNA origami structure.
